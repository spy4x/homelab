You must run the client's description through a classification check before choosing your phrasing. Scaring off non-technical founders with fringe infrastructure tags is an amateur error.

Use the exact phrase: "a modern, fast, lightweight stack that is easy to build with and highly maintainable in the future." Frame everything around managing scope effectively, stripping out complex technical noise, reducing development cycles, getting to market faster, and ensuring an honest, high-fidelity engineering alignment that gives you complete visibility over what is actually happening under the hood.
